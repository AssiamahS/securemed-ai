Frequently Asked Questions

Is SecureMed actually HIPAA compliant?
SecureMed is designed for HIPAA-aligned deployments. Because the AI runs entirely on your infrastructure, PHI never leaves your network — eliminating the biggest compliance risk. We include encryption at rest (KMS), audit logging, role-based access control, and VPC isolation. You maintain full control, which simplifies your compliance posture significantly.
Do I need a BAA with SecureMed?
No. BAAs are required when a third party handles your PHI. Since SecureMed runs on your own infrastructure, there's no third-party access to your data. If deployed on AWS, you should have a BAA with AWS (available free via AWS Artifact) for the underlying compute.
How does the AI quality compare to ChatGPT or Claude?
For focused medical tasks — summarization, document analysis, differential diagnosis — open models like Qwen 2.5 perform comparably. For general conversation, cloud models are stronger. The tradeoff: you get complete data privacy and no per-user costs. Enterprise clients can also fine-tune models on their own medical data for superior domain performance.
What hardware do I need?
Starter: Any machine with 16GB+ RAM (runs locally on Mac or Linux). Professional: AWS t3.xlarge (16GB RAM, ~$120/mo) or equivalent. Enterprise: We size hardware to your workload. A mini PC in your server closet works for small clinics.
Can I switch AI models?
Yes. SecureMed runs on Ollama, which supports dozens of open models. Switch from Qwen to Llama, Mistral, or a medical-tuned model with one command. No vendor lock-in.
What does deployment look like?
Professional plan includes Terraform scripts that deploy your entire HIPAA-compliant infrastructure in under 30 minutes: encrypted VPC, private subnets, the AI server, ELK monitoring, CloudTrail — everything. One command. If it breaks, one command rebuilds it identically.
